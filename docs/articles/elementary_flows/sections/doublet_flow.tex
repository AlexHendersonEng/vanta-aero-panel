\section{Doublet Flow}
\label{sec:doublet-flow}

A doublet is obtained by bringing a source and sink of equal strength
together while maintaining a finite product

\[
\mu = Qd
\]

where $d$ is the separation distance.

\bigskip

The resulting velocity field is

\[
V_r
=
-\frac{\mu\cos\theta}{2\pi r^2},
\qquad
V_\theta
=
-\frac{\mu\sin\theta}{2\pi r^2}
\]

\subsection{Velocity Potential}
\label{subsec:doubler-flow-velocity-potential}

The velocity potential is

\[
\boxed{
\phi
=
-\frac{\mu}{2\pi r}\cos\theta
}
\]

\subsection{Stream Function}
\label{subsec:doublet-flow-stream-function}

The stream function is

\[
\boxed{
\psi
=
-\frac{\mu}{2\pi r}\sin\theta
}
\]
